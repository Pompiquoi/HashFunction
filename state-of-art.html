State of Art

In recent years, the cryptographic community has focused on strengthening hash functions due to increasing computational attacks. Here are the key highlights of the current landscape:

Widely Used Hash Functions

  • MD5: One of the oldest hash functions, now considered broken due to its vulnerability to collision attacks.
  • SHA-1: Used extensively in the past but deprecated after researchers demonstrated practical collision attacks.
  • SHA-2: A family of more secure algorithms, including SHA-256 and SHA-512, widely adopted in secure systems today.
  • SHA-3: The latest standard, selected through an open competition by NIST, designed to address the shortcomings of SHA-2.

Emerging Trends

With the rise of quantum computing, there is growing interest in post-quantum cryptography. Researchers are developing hash functions that are resistant to quantum attacks, ensuring long-term security.
